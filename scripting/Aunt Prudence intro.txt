"Prudence" is hilariously ironic for an aunt who enjoys her liquor a little too much, and "Clutterbuck" is just the right mix of bumbling and old-timey charm. Aunt Prue is the type to insist she’s “only had a sip” while dramatically knocking over a decanter, telling outrageous (and likely exaggerated) stories of her youth, and swearing she once danced with Prince Albert—though no one believes her.
